Hoychoy Cafe

End-to-End Hyperlocal Delivery App

Client Hoychoy Cafe (Golaghat, Assam)
Role UI/UX Designer & Developer
Timeline Shipped 2026

1. Context & Overview

The Story

In the small town of Golaghat, Assam, working professionals—especially those at companies like ONGC—frequently face a dilemma: a lack of reliable food delivery. Major aggregators like Zomato and Swiggy are unavailable, leaving a gap for accessible, fast, and digital-first food ordering.

The Problem

Hoychoy Cafe needed a direct-to-consumer digital ordering experience. The solution had to be frictionless, requiring zero app downloads or logins, while offering a modern, premium feel that working professionals expect.

The Goal

To design and build an easy-to-use hyperlocal web application from scratch—handling everything from menu browsing and dynamic distance-based delivery calculations to final UPI payment and automated order transmission.

2. UX Strategy & Flow

Discover → Select → Pay

The user journey was rigorously streamlined to minimize friction and maximize conversions:

  1. Immediate Menu Access: Users land directly on the menu. No signup walls, no tedious onboarding.
  2. Clear Availability: Out-of-stock items are visually disabled immediately, preventing user frustration.
  3. Frictionless Checkout: The app captures precise GPS coordinates (or accepts manual Google Maps links) to calculate exact Haversine delivery distances, ensuring fair and automated delivery pricing.

5. Engineering & "Vibe Coding"

This project wasn't just designed; it was built end-to-end. It exemplifies the power of Vibe Coding—using rapid, intent-driven, AI-assisted development (React, Vite, TailwindCSS) to move from UX concept to shipping logic.

Highlights

  • Dynamic Distance Logic: Instead of drawing static polygons, I implemented real-time Haversine footprint checks to calculate distance-tiered delivery (e.g., ₹80 for <5km).
  • Stateless Architecture: Rather than building a heavy backend, the app creates a "Snapshot" of the user's cart alongside coordinates, parsing it neatly into an automated WhatsApp string.
  • The `/admin` Dashboard: Designed and coded an internal admin panel to let staff toggle item availability, print tickets, and track UPI references.

Why This Matters

Building Hoychoy Cafe required bridging the gap between designing a beautiful interface and engineering a working, resilient product. It proves an ability to own the entire product life cycle—from empathetic user research in Golaghat down to shipping the final React component that processes a payment.
